# Oatmeal

Oatmeal is a nutritious breakfast staple made from ground or rolled oats. It's versatile, healthy, and forms the foundation of countless delicious meals.

## Types of oats

### Steel-cut oats
Also known as Irish oats, these are whole oat groats chopped into pieces. They have a chewy texture and nutty flavor.

**Cooking time:** 20-30 minutes

### Rolled oats (old-fashioned)
Oat groats that have been steamed and rolled flat. They cook faster than steel-cut oats while maintaining good texture.

**Cooking time:** 5-10 minutes

### Quick oats
Rolled oats that have been cut into smaller pieces for faster cooking. They have a softer texture than rolled oats.

**Cooking time:** 1-2 minutes

### Instant oats
Pre-cooked and dried oats that just need hot water. Often flavored and sweetened.

**Cooking time:** 30 seconds to 1 minute

## Basic cooking methods

### Stovetop method

1. Combine 1 cup oats with 2 cups liquid (water, milk, or combination)
2. Add a pinch of salt
3. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low
4. Simmer until desired consistency is reached
5. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking

### Microwave method

1. Combine 1/2 cup oats with 1 cup liquid in microwave-safe bowl
2. Microwave on high for 2-3 minutes
3. Stir and let stand for 1 minute before serving

### Overnight oats (no-cook)

1. Mix equal parts oats and liquid in a jar
2. Add sweeteners and flavors
3. Refrigerate overnight
4. Serve cold with toppings

## Nutritional benefits

Oatmeal provides numerous health benefits:

- **High in fiber**: Supports digestive health and helps maintain stable blood sugar
- **Protein source**: Contains all essential amino acids
- **Heart healthy**: Beta-glucan fiber helps lower cholesterol
- **Sustained energy**: Complex carbohydrates provide long-lasting fuel
- **Vitamins and minerals**: Rich in manganese, phosphorus, magnesium, and B vitamins

## Tips for perfect oatmeal

<Tip>
Use a 1:2 ratio of oats to liquid for creamy oatmeal, or 1:1.5 for thicker consistency.
</Tip>

- Toast oats in a dry pan before cooking for enhanced nutty flavor
- Add salt to enhance the overall taste
- Stir in milk or cream at the end for extra creaminess
- Prepare toppings while oats are cooking to save time

## Storage and meal prep

### Cooked oatmeal
- Refrigerate for up to 5 days
- Reheat with additional liquid to restore creamy texture
- Freeze portions for up to 3 months

### Dry oats
- Store in airtight containers for up to 2 years
- Keep in cool, dry place away from light
- Whole oats last longer than processed varieties

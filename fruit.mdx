## Overview

Fruit is the seed-bearing structure in flowering plants that is formed from the ovary after flowering. Fruits are an important source of dietary fiber, vitamins, and antioxidants.

## Types of fruit

### Citrus fruits

Citrus fruits are known for their high vitamin C content and tangy flavor.

- **Oranges** - Sweet and juicy, rich in vitamin C
- **Lemons** - Tart and acidic, used in cooking and beverages
- **Limes** - Similar to lemons but with a distinct flavor
- **Grapefruits** - Larger citrus fruit with a slightly bitter taste

### Berries

Berries are small, pulpy, and often edible fruits.

- **Strawberries** - Sweet red berries, popular in desserts
- **Blueberries** - Small blue fruits packed with antioxidants
- **Raspberries** - Delicate red berries with a sweet-tart flavor
- **Blackberries** - Dark purple berries with a rich flavor

### Tropical fruits

Tropical fruits grow in warm climates and offer unique flavors.

- **Bananas** - High in potassium and easy to digest
- **Mangoes** - Sweet and juicy with a distinctive flavor
- **Pineapples** - Tangy and sweet, contains bromelain enzyme
- **Papayas** - Soft orange flesh, rich in vitamins A and C

## Storage tips

<Tip>
  Store most fruits at room temperature until ripe, then refrigerate to extend freshness.
</Tip>

<Warning>
  Some fruits like bananas and avocados should not be refrigerated until fully ripe, as cold temperatures can prevent proper ripening.
</Warning>

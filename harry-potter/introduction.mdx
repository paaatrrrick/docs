## Overview

Harry Potter is a fantasy series written by British author J.K. Rowling. The story follows Harry Potter, a young wizard who discovers his magical heritage on his eleventh birthday and attends Hogwarts School of Witchcraft and Wizardry.

## Key facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Author | J.K. Rowling |
| First book | Harry Potter and the Philosopher's Stone (1997) |
| Film adaptation | Warner Bros. (2001-2011) |
| Setting | United Kingdom (wizarding world) |
| Books | 7 |

## Hogwarts houses

- **Gryffindor** — Values bravery, daring, and chivalry
- **Slytherin** — Values ambition, cunning, and resourcefulness
- **Ravenclaw** — Values intelligence, wisdom, and creativity
- **Hufflepuff** — Values hard work, patience, and loyalty

## Themes

<Note>
"It does not do to dwell on dreams and forget to live." — Albus Dumbledore
</Note>

The series explores themes of:

- Good versus evil
- Love and sacrifice
- Friendship and loyalty
- The power of choice

## Main characters

- **Harry Potter** — The Boy Who Lived, protagonist of the series
- **Hermione Granger** — Brilliant witch and Harry's best friend
- **Ron Weasley** — Loyal friend from a large wizarding family
- **Albus Dumbledore** — Headmaster of Hogwarts
- **Lord Voldemort** — The Dark Lord, main antagonist

## Legacy

Harry Potter became a global cultural phenomenon, inspiring theme parks, a play, spin-off films, and generations of readers to discover the magic of reading.

## Overview

Game of Thrones is an epic fantasy series based on George R.R. Martin's "A Song of Ice and Fire" novels. The story follows the power struggles among noble families as they fight for control of the Iron Throne.

## Key facts

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Author | George R.R. Martin |
| First book | A Game of Thrones (1996) |
| TV adaptation | HBO (2011-2019) |
| Setting | Westeros and Essos |
| Seasons | 8 |

## Major houses

- **House Stark** — "Winter Is Coming" — Lords of Winterfell
- **House Lannister** — "Hear Me Roar" — Lords of Casterly Rock
- **House Targaryen** — "Fire and Blood" — Exiled rulers of the Seven Kingdoms
- **House Baratheon** — "Ours Is the Fury" — Lords of Storm's End

## Themes

<Note>
"When you play the game of thrones, you win or you die." — Cersei Lannister
</Note>

The series explores themes of:

- Power and corruption
- Honor and betrayal
- Family loyalty and duty
- The consequences of war

## The Iron Throne

The Iron Throne is the seat of power in the Seven Kingdoms. Forged from the swords of Aegon the Conqueror's enemies, it represents absolute rule over Westeros. The struggle for this throne drives much of the series' conflict.

## Legacy

Game of Thrones became a global phenomenon, redefining television fantasy and proving that complex, morally ambiguous storytelling could achieve mainstream success.

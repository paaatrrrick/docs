## Overview

Pigs are highly intelligent animals that belong to the Suidae family. They are known for their remarkable cognitive abilities, social behavior, and adaptability to various environments.

## Physical characteristics

Pigs typically have:
- A stout, barrel-shaped body
- A short, flexible snout for rooting and foraging
- Four-toed hooves with two main weight-bearing toes
- Sparse hair covering their skin
- Weight ranging from 50 to 700 pounds depending on breed

## Intelligence and behavior

Pigs are among the most intelligent animals on Earth. They demonstrate:

- **Problem-solving abilities**: Pigs can navigate complex mazes and solve puzzles
- **Social intelligence**: They form complex social hierarchies and recognize individual relationships
- **Communication**: Pigs use over 20 different vocalizations to communicate
- **Memory**: They can remember locations and associate them with positive or negative experiences
- **Tool use**: Some pigs have been observed using tools to accomplish tasks

## Diet and feeding

Pigs are omnivores with diverse dietary needs:
- Roots, fruits, and vegetables
- Insects and small animals
- Commercial feed in farming operations
- They use their snouts to root through soil searching for food

## Importance to humans

### Agriculture
- Primary source of pork, bacon, and ham worldwide
- Efficient converters of feed to meat
- Important economic livestock in many countries

### Medical research
- Pig organs are similar in size to human organs
- Used in xenotransplantation research
- Insulin was historically derived from pigs

### Environmental benefits
- Help with land management through rooting behavior
- Can clear underbrush and prepare soil

## Breeds

Popular pig breeds include:
- **Yorkshire**: Large, white pigs known for meat production
- **Hampshire**: Black pigs with white belt, excellent mothers
- **Duroc**: Red-colored pigs with good meat quality
- **Berkshire**: Black pigs with white markings, premium meat

## Conservation status

While domestic pigs are abundant, some wild pig species face conservation challenges:
- Wild boar populations are stable in most regions
- Some subspecies like the Visayan warty pig are endangered
- Habitat loss remains a primary threat to wild pig species

## Fun facts

- Pigs cannot sweat, so they roll in mud to cool down
- They have excellent long-term memories
- Pigs are naturally clean animals when given adequate space
- They can run up to 11 miles per hour
- A group of pigs is called a drift, drove, or team

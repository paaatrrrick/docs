# Dog care

Proper care is essential for your dog's health, happiness, and longevity. This guide covers the fundamentals of dog care.

## Daily care essentials

### Feeding

**Feeding schedule:**
- Puppies (8-16 weeks): 3-4 meals per day
- Adult dogs (1+ years): 2 meals per day
- Senior dogs (7+ years): 2 smaller meals per day

**Nutrition tips:**
- Choose high-quality dog food appropriate for your dog's age and size
- Measure portions to prevent obesity
- Provide fresh water at all times
- Avoid toxic foods like chocolate, grapes, and onions

<Warning>
Never feed your dog chocolate, grapes, raisins, onions, garlic, or xylitol-containing products. These can be toxic or fatal.
</Warning>

### Exercise

**Daily exercise requirements:**
- Small breeds: 30 minutes to 1 hour
- Medium breeds: 1-2 hours
- Large breeds: 2+ hours
- Working breeds: 2-3+ hours

**Exercise ideas:**
- Walking and jogging
- Playing fetch
- Swimming
- Agility training
- Dog parks for socialization

### Grooming

**Regular grooming tasks:**

| Task | Frequency | Purpose |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Brushing | Daily to weekly | Prevent matting, reduce shedding |
| Bathing | Monthly or as needed | Cleanliness and skin health |
| Nail trimming | Every 2-3 weeks | Comfort and proper walking |
| Teeth brushing | Daily | Dental health |
| Ear cleaning | Weekly | Prevent infections |

## Creating a safe environment

### Dog-proofing your home

- Secure toxic substances (cleaning supplies, medications)
- Remove small objects that could be swallowed
- Install baby gates to restrict access to certain areas
- Ensure electrical cords are out of reach
- Check that plants are non-toxic to dogs

### Outdoor safety

- Secure fencing in your yard
- Use a leash in public areas
- Provide shade and water during hot weather
- Check for hazards like toxic plants or chemicals

## Health monitoring

### Signs of good health

- Clear, bright eyes
- Clean ears without odor
- Healthy skin and shiny coat
- Regular eating and drinking
- Normal energy levels
- Regular bowel movements

### Warning signs to watch for

<Accordion title="When to call your vet">
  Contact your veterinarian if you notice:
  - Loss of appetite for more than 24 hours
  - Vomiting or diarrhea
  - Difficulty breathing
  - Excessive lethargy
  - Changes in urination habits
  - Limping or difficulty moving
  - Unusual behaviors or personality changes
</Accordion>

## Socialization

Proper socialization is crucial, especially for puppies:

- Introduce your dog to different people, animals, and environments
- Start socialization early (3-16 weeks for puppies)
- Use positive experiences and rewards
- Enroll in puppy classes
- Regular exposure to various situations builds confidence

## Emergency preparedness

### First aid kit essentials

- Gauze and bandages
- Antiseptic wipes
- Thermometer
- Emergency vet contact information
- Hydrogen peroxide (for induced vomiting, only if instructed by a vet)

### Emergency contacts

Keep readily available:
- Your veterinarian's contact information
- Emergency animal hospital details
- Pet poison control hotline: (888) 426-4435

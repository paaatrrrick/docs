# Dog health

Maintaining your dog's health requires regular veterinary care, preventive measures, and awareness of common health issues.

## Preventive care

### Regular veterinary checkups

**Recommended schedule:**
- Puppies: Every 3-4 weeks until 16 weeks old
- Adult dogs (1-7 years): Annual checkups
- Senior dogs (7+ years): Bi-annual checkups

**What to expect during checkups:**
- Physical examination
- Weight monitoring
- Vaccination updates
- Parasite screening
- Dental assessment
- Discussion of diet and exercise

### Vaccinations

**Core vaccines (recommended for all dogs):**
- Rabies
- DHPP (Distemper, Hepatitis, Parvovirus, Parainfluenza)

**Non-core vaccines (based on lifestyle and risk):**
- Bordetella (kennel cough)
- Lyme disease
- Canine influenza
- Leptospirosis

<Tip>
Discuss your dog's lifestyle with your vet to determine which non-core vaccines are appropriate.
</Tip>

### Parasite prevention

**Internal parasites:**
- Heartworms: Monthly preventive medication
- Intestinal worms: Regular fecal exams and deworming

**External parasites:**
- Fleas: Year-round prevention recommended
- Ticks: Especially important in tick-endemic areas
- Regular checks after outdoor activities

## Common health issues

### Obesity

**Signs of obesity:**
- Inability to feel ribs easily
- No visible waist when viewed from above
- Difficulty breathing or exercising
- Lethargy

**Prevention and management:**
- Measure food portions carefully
- Limit treats to 10% of daily calories
- Provide regular exercise
- Consult your vet for weight management plans

### Dental disease

**Signs of dental problems:**
- Bad breath
- Yellow or brown tartar buildup
- Red, swollen gums
- Difficulty eating
- Pawing at the face

**Prevention:**
- Daily teeth brushing
- Dental chews and toys
- Regular professional cleanings
- Annual dental examinations

### Skin conditions

**Common skin issues:**
- Allergies (food, environmental)
- Hot spots
- Dry skin
- Parasites

**When to see a vet:**
- Persistent itching or scratching
- Hair loss
- Red, inflamed skin
- Unusual odors

## Age-related health considerations

### Puppy health

**Key focus areas:**
- Vaccination schedule
- Spaying/neutering timing
- Growth monitoring
- Socialization period health

**Common puppy issues:**
- Parasites
- Hypoglycemia (small breeds)
- Retained baby teeth
- Growth plate injuries

### Senior dog health

**Age-related changes:**
- Decreased mobility
- Vision and hearing changes
- Cognitive changes
- Organ function decline

**Senior care tips:**
- More frequent vet visits
- Joint support supplements
- Adjusted exercise routines
- Comfortable sleeping areas

## Emergency situations

### Signs requiring immediate veterinary attention

<Accordion title="Emergency symptoms">
  Seek immediate veterinary care if your dog shows:
  - Difficulty breathing
  - Seizures
  - Collapse or loss of consciousness
  - Severe vomiting or diarrhea
  - Inability to urinate or defecate
  - Suspected poisoning
  - Severe pain or injury
  - Bloated, distended abdomen
  - Pale gums
  - Extreme lethargy
</Accordion>

### First aid basics

**Before going to the vet:**
- Remain calm
- Safely restrain your dog if necessary
- Apply pressure to bleeding wounds
- Keep your dog warm and comfortable
- Contact your vet or emergency clinic

**What NOT to do:**
- Give human medications
- Induce vomiting unless instructed by a vet
- Move a severely injured dog unnecessarily
- Panic - dogs can sense stress

## Nutrition and health

### Life stage nutrition

**Puppies:**
- High-calorie, nutrient-dense puppy food
- Multiple small meals throughout the day
- Transition to adult food around 12-18 months

**Adult dogs:**
- Balanced adult maintenance diet
- Regular feeding schedule
- Monitor weight and adjust portions as needed

**Senior dogs:**
- Senior formula with joint support
- Easily digestible proteins
- May need smaller, more frequent meals

### Supplements

**Commonly beneficial supplements:**
- Omega-3 fatty acids for coat and joint health
- Glucosamine and chondroitin for joint support
- Probiotics for digestive health

<Warning>
Always consult your veterinarian before adding supplements to your dog's diet.
</Warning>

## Monitoring your dog's health at home

### Daily health checks

- Monitor eating and drinking habits
- Check energy levels and behavior
- Observe bathroom habits
- Look for any physical changes

### Weekly health checks

- Check ears for odor or discharge
- Examine eyes for clarity and discharge
- Feel for lumps or bumps
- Check paws for cuts or irritation
- Assess coat condition

### Keeping health records

Maintain records of:
- Vaccination dates
- Medication schedules
- Weight changes
- Behavioral changes
- Vet visit notes

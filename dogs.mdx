## About dogs

Dogs are domesticated mammals and loyal companions that have been with humans for thousands of years. They are known for their intelligence, loyalty, and diverse range of breeds.

## Popular dog breeds

Here are some of the most popular dog breeds and their characteristics:

### Golden Retriever
- **Size**: Large
- **Temperament**: Friendly, intelligent, devoted
- **Good for**: Families, active owners

### Labrador Retriever
- **Size**: Large
- **Temperament**: Outgoing, active, loyal
- **Good for**: Families, first-time owners

### German Shepherd
- **Size**: Large
- **Temperament**: Confident, courageous, smart
- **Good for**: Experienced owners, working roles

### Bulldog
- **Size**: Medium
- **Temperament**: Docile, willful, friendly
- **Good for**: Apartment living, less active owners

## Dog care essentials

Taking care of a dog requires commitment and preparation:

<Card title="Daily needs" icon="heart">
  Dogs need daily exercise, fresh water, nutritious food, and companionship.
</Card>

### Exercise requirements
- **Small breeds**: 30-60 minutes daily
- **Medium breeds**: 60-90 minutes daily
- **Large breeds**: 90-120 minutes daily

### Nutrition
Feed your dog high-quality dog food appropriate for their:
- Age (puppy, adult, senior)
- Size (small, medium, large)
- Activity level
- Any special health needs

## Training basics

Start training early for the best results:

1. **House training**: Establish a routine for potty breaks
2. **Basic commands**: Teach sit, stay, come, and down
3. **Socialization**: Expose your dog to different people, animals, and environments
4. **Positive reinforcement**: Use treats and praise to reward good behavior

<Warning>
  Never use punishment-based training methods, as they can harm your relationship with your dog and cause behavioral issues.
</Warning>

## Health considerations

Regular veterinary care is essential:

- **Vaccinations**: Keep up with required vaccines
- **Parasite prevention**: Use flea, tick, and heartworm preventatives
- **Dental care**: Brush teeth regularly and provide dental chews
- **Regular checkups**: Annual or bi-annual vet visits

## Fun facts about dogs

- Dogs have an incredible sense of smell that is 10,000-100,000 times better than humans
- They can learn over 150 words and can count to 4 or 5
- A dog's nose print is unique, just like a human fingerprint
- Dogs dream similarly to humans and can experience REM sleep

<Card title="Did you know?" icon="lightbulb">
  The average dog lives 10-14 years, but this varies significantly by breed and size.
</Card>

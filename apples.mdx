## Overview

Apples are one of the most popular and nutritious fruits worldwide. With hundreds of varieties available, they offer diverse flavors, textures, and uses in cooking and nutrition.

## Popular varieties

Learn about the most common apple varieties and their characteristics.

### Red Delicious

A classic sweet apple with:
- Deep red skin
- Mild, sweet flavor
- Soft texture
- Best for eating fresh

### Granny Smith

A tart green apple featuring:
- Bright green skin
- Sharp, tangy flavor
- Crisp texture
- Excellent for baking

### Gala

A sweet and crisp variety with:
- Yellow-red striped skin
- Mild, sweet taste
- Firm texture
- Great for snacking

### Honeycrisp

A modern favorite offering:
- Red and yellow skin
- Sweet-tart balance
- Exceptionally crisp texture
- Perfect for fresh eating

## Storage and selection

### Choosing fresh apples

When selecting apples:
- Look for firm, unblemished skin
- Avoid soft spots or bruises
- Choose apples with intact stems
- Smell for fresh, sweet aroma

### Storage tips

To keep apples fresh:

```markdown
Refrigerator: 1-2 months in crisper drawer
Counter: 1-2 weeks at room temperature
Freezer: Slice and freeze for up to 8 months
```

## Growing apples

Learn the basics of apple cultivation:

### Climate requirements

Apple trees thrive in:
- Temperate climates with cold winters
- Areas with 500-1000+ chill hours
- Well-drained soil with pH 6.0-7.0

### Care and maintenance

Essential care includes:
- Regular pruning in late winter
- Adequate watering during growing season
- Pest and disease management
- Annual fertilization

<Warning>
Apple trees typically require cross-pollination with another variety to produce fruit.
</Warning>

## Fun facts

Interesting apple trivia:

- There are over 7,500 known apple varieties worldwide
- Apples are 25% air, which is why they float
- The average person eats about 65 apples per year
- Apple trees can live and produce fruit for over 100 years
- "An apple a day keeps the doctor away" originated in Wales in the 1860s

<Tip>
Store apples away from other fruits to prevent them from ripening too quickly due to ethylene gas production.
</Tip>
